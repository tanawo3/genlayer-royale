What is GenLayer?

A Protocol That Uses AI to Resolve Disputes and Enforce Digital Contracts

GenLayer is an AI-native trust layer and synthetic jurisdiction on-chain. Validators running diverse language models act as a decentralized digital court, resolving disputes and enforcing contracts. Intelligent Contracts interpret language, process unstructured data, and pull live web inputs, enabling autonomous systems to transact, govern, and settle decisions at machine speed.

Subjective Decisions

Evaluate context and nuance. Turn judgment calls into enforceable on-chain outcomes.

Unstructured Data

Process text, images, and qualitative evidence. Handle real-world complexity natively.

Internet Access

Fetch live web data directly on-chain. No oracles, no intermediaries, no delays.

Use cases

Programmable Trust for the Real World

By embedding AI at the consensus layer, GenLayer redefines what's possible on-chain. Developers can write contracts in natural language, integrate real-world data directly, and build applications that interpret events, evaluate evidence, and adapt to changing conditions - all executed trustlessly through AI consensus.

Autonomous Escrow Services

AI agents negotiate terms, verify fulfillment conditions, and release cross-border payments - all without human intervention or traditional banking infrastructure.

Instant Marketplace Arbitration

Validator consensus settles buyer-seller disputes in seconds by evaluating evidence, contract terms, and contextual data - delivering impartial verdicts at scale.

Real-Time Prediction Markets

Markets self-settle by fetching and verifying outcomes directly from primary sources. No centralized resolution authority or manual intervention required.

AI-Governed DAOs

Natural language proposals are analyzed against real-time data and automatically executed when conditions are met - enabling truly autonomous organizations.

Automated Compliance Screening

Real-time verification against sanctions lists, KYC/AML requirements - creating compliant tokenized assets without manual processes.

Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)

Provide decentralized, AI-powered arbitration for commercial contracts, cutting cost, delay, and bias from the legal stack.

Subjective Contract Automation

AI consensus interprets ambiguous terms such as "legal" or "force majeure", turning subjective language into deterministic execution.
